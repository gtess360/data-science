Task 1

EDA Findings

Key insights discovered during exploratory data analysis across 25 independent variables.

Price Distribution
Price is right-skewed (skewness = 1.63). Most cars are priced between $5,000–$15,000 with a long tail of luxury vehicles up to $45,400.
Missing Values
normalized-losses had 18% missing values — the highest of any column. Handled via median imputation. Values were stored as '?' requiring special load handling.
Multicollinearity
city-mpg and highway-mpg were correlated at 0.97 — nearly identical information. city-mpg was dropped, highway-mpg retained.
Feature Selection
From 25 original features only 5 were selected by the model — engine-size, curb-weight, horsepower, highway-mpg and width. These 5 features explain 92% of price variation, making the model simpler and more accurate.
Engine Size
Engine size has the strongest positive correlation with price (0.87). Larger engines consistently command higher prices across all makes.
Fuel Efficiency vs Price
City and highway mpg show negative correlation with price — more expensive cars have lower fuel efficiency due to larger, more powerful engines.
